Nice — yes, with **Code Interpreter** capability turned on (the toggle in Agent Builder under "Capabilities"), the agent can actually generate the .pptx file directly. That changes the game.

Here are demo prompts designed to make each agent sing in front of an audience. I've picked ones that force cross-cell reasoning so the output feels intelligent, not lookup-y.

## Agent 6 — Market Research Analyst

Upload `6_Market_Research_Survey.xlsx`, then try:

1. **"Analyze the survey data and build me an 8-slide market opportunity deck. Identify our top 2 target segments based on willingness to switch, satisfaction with current solution, and budget. Include a competitor positioning slide. Generate the PowerPoint."**

2. **"Build a board deck recommending where we should focus our 2026 GTM investment. Use the survey data to defend the recommendation with specific numbers. Generate the PowerPoint."**

3. **"Compare Healthcare and Finance segments side-by-side — satisfaction, top features wanted, willingness to switch, and average budget. Build a 5-slide deck arguing which segment we should prioritize. Generate the PowerPoint."**

*Why these land:* All three force the agent to combine multiple columns and hit the engineered insight (Finance has 2.88 satisfaction and 100% are at least open to switching — that's the obvious answer, but the agent has to *find* it).

## Agent 7 — Quarterly Performance Analyst

Upload `7_Quarterly_Revenue_Data.xlsx`, then try:

1. **"Build me an 8-slide investor-ready deck on our FY 2025 performance. Include our Rule of 40 score vs each peer, call out our breakout segment and our laggard, and end with a forward-look slide. Generate the PowerPoint."**

2. **"Create a board pack with one slide for the BLUF, one for top-line, one for segment performance, two for peer benchmarking, and one for the operational health story (NRR, churn, NPS). Quantify everywhere. Generate the PowerPoint."**

3. **"Build a 'highlights and lowlights' deck for our quarterly review — 3 wins with numbers, 3 watch-outs with numbers, and 2 actions for Q1 2026. Generate the PowerPoint."**

*Why these land:* The agent has to discover that AI Studio grew from $4.5M → $28M (the breakout), Storage is declining (the laggard), and our Rule of 40 lags CloudOne despite faster growth. Those three findings together are a real analyst's insight, not a lookup.

## Agent 8 — Contract Extraction Agent

Upload `9_Sample_Contract_MSA.docx`, then try:

1. **"Extract all key terms from this contract into a structured table. Flag any clauses that deviate from market standard or favor the counterparty. Generate a PowerPoint summary with one slide each for: parties & dates, commercial terms, risk flags, and recommended actions before signing."**

2. **"What deadlines am I in danger of missing if I don't track this contract carefully? Give me a calendar of every notice window, payment cycle, and renewal trigger. Generate a PowerPoint with the dates as a timeline visual."**

3. **"Pretend I'm the General Counsel reviewing this in 5 minutes. Build me a 4-slide executive briefing: what is this, what are the top 3 risks, what's non-standard, and what's my recommendation. Generate the PowerPoint."**

*Why these land:* Question 2 is the killer — it surfaces the **60-day renewal notice window** (easy to miss), the **Net 60 payment cycle**, and the **180-day vendor termination notice** as a date-based risk profile. Most legal reviewers wouldn't catch all three on a fast read.

---

**One workshop tip:** for each agent, ask the same question *twice in a row* with a small variation ("now do it for 6 slides instead of 8" or "now focus only on EMEA"). The audience seeing the agent re-run with a tweaked angle is the moment where it stops feeling like a one-trick demo and starts feeling like a real virtual analyst.
